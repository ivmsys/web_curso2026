Lecture Highlights

  • Robotics overview – surveys industrial, mobile, humanoid, aerial, marine and wearable robotics and illustrates modern applications.
  • Underactuated systems – studies control of systems with fewer actuators than degrees of freedom, including stabilization and limit cycle generation.
  • Parallel manipulators – compares serial and parallel robots, presenting adaptive control solutions for high‑speed applications.
  • Autonomous underwater vehicles – examines nonlinear dynamics and adaptive control strategies for tethered vehicles.
  • Humanoid gait – introduces human‑data‑based control for dynamic walking using foot and centre‑of‑mass positions.
  • Wearable robotics – discusses demographic drivers and control solutions for rehabilitation exoskeletons.
  • Laboratory course – offers hands‑on simulations for various robotic systems using MATLAB/Simulink.

Keynote

Dr Ahmed Chemori received his M.Sc. and Ph.D. degrees in automatic control from the Polytechnic Institute of Grenoble in 2001 and 2005. He is a senior researcher at CNRS (LIRMM, University of Montpellier) and an expert in nonlinear control with real‑time applications in parallel robotics, underwater robotics, wearable robotics and underactuated systems. With more than 190 publications and extensive experience supervising students, he serves as a technical editor for IEEE/ASME Transactions on Mechatronics and contributes to several international technical committees. Dr Chemori will deliver the majority of lectures during the school.

Panel & Special Sessions

  • From theory to real‑world applications – Dr Mauricio Corona Chavez will demonstrate how to deploy and sustain connected complex robots in industrial settings.
  • Demonstrations session – Hipolito Aguilar Sierra will host live demonstrations of autonomous systems and technical walkthroughs.
  • Cultural event: Friendly robot match – an informal contest where participants interact with robots in a friendly competition.

Poster Session

Each day features a coffee break combined with a poster session. Researchers and students are invited to showcase their work on topics such as motion control, nonlinear control, wearable robotics, underwater robotics, parallel manipulators, human–robot interaction and AI for complex robots. Guidelines for poster dimensions and submission deadlines will be provided. The best poster will be recognised during the closing ceremony.
